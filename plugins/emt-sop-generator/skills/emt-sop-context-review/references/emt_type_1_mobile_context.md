# EMT Type 1 Mobile — Agent Context

_Source: WHO Classification and Minimum Standards for EMTs, 2021_

---

## Identity Parameters

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Type | EMT Type 1 Mobile |
| Facility model | Mobile outpatient initial care team working in multiple locations |
| Care level | Daylight-hours stabilization of acute trauma and non-trauma presentations, referral, and community-based primary care |
| Min throughput | At least 50 outpatients/day per mobile team |
| Operating hours | Daytime only; shortened clinic times must allow safe travel to/from remote sites |
| Deployment readiness | Operational from arrival to affected area/site within 24 h; all EMTs expected to be deployable and operational within 72 h of disaster |
| Min deployment duration | 2 weeks |
| Structure | Light, portable, adaptable mobile outpatient facility; may use existing structures, tents, or specially equipped vehicles; requires a base of operations for resupply, self-sufficiency, sterility, cold chain, and supply chain |

---

## Scope of Care

**Permitted:**
- Field triage, assessment, first aid, basic resuscitation, stabilization, and referral
- Treatment of basic trauma and non-trauma emergencies in an outpatient/mobile setting
- Community-based primary care for basic communicable diseases and NCDs
- Basic reproductive health services and B-EmONC within outpatient/mobile capability
- Basic paediatric care, nutrition screening, initial palliative care, and basic rehabilitation
- Minor procedures under local anaesthesia when sterility and pain control can be ensured

**Refer to higher care:**
- Any patient requiring inpatient care, surgery, advanced airway, intensive care, blood transfusion, imaging, or specialist diagnostics
- Burns beyond first aid/pain relief or requiring definitive treatment
- Fractures, spinal injuries, wounds, obstetric complications, severe paediatric illness, SAM with complications, or NCD exacerbations beyond outpatient capability
- Suspected communicable disease requiring isolation or inpatient management

---

## Explicit Exclusions

- No inpatient beds or ward management
- No operating theatre, general surgery, orthopaedic surgery, obstetric surgery, or caesarean section
- No advanced airway management, endotracheal intubation, surgical airway, or mechanical ventilation
- No diagnostic imaging as a minimum standard
- No blood transfusion capability
- No ICU or critical care admission
- No definitive burn, fracture, spinal, or complex wound care
- No temporary morgue requirement at mobile clinic sites

## Clinical Capabilities

| Domain | Capability |
|---|---|
| Triage | Initial and field triage; unique patient identifier; MCI triage system |
| Resuscitation | Basic resuscitation and stabilization; BLS for adult, child, and neonate; oxygen, BVM, IV access/fluids, haemorrhage control, hypothermia prevention |
| Airway | Basic airway adjuncts only: nasopharyngeal/oropharyngeal airway, BVM, oxygen; no intubation |
| Wounds | Initial wound assessment, cleaning, dressing, tetanus prophylaxis, antibiotics when indicated, referral for debridement/closure requiring surgery |
| Burns | Burns first aid and pain relief; refer for definitive burns care |
| Fractures | Basic fracture management, immobilization/back-slab, pain relief, referral if surgery or follow-up is required |
| Spinal injury | Assessment, immobilization, pressure-area precautions, transfer; report suspected spinal injury |
| Communicable disease | Screening, identification, case definition use, temporary separation where possible, referral and reporting |
| NCDs | Basic outpatient chronic disease care; urgent referral for severe/life-threatening exacerbations; support treatment continuity |
| RMNH | B-EmONC and sexual/reproductive health; uncomplicated delivery support only if within team competence; refer complications |
| Paediatrics | Basic outpatient paediatric care, stabilization, essential newborn care, nutrition screening, child protection awareness |
| Analgesia/anaesthesia | Local anaesthesia and pain control only |
| Surgery | Minor outpatient procedures with local anaesthesia only |
| Palliative care | Initial palliative care, pain/symptom relief, counselling, referral/continuity plan |
| Rehabilitation | Basic rehabilitation care and referral for ongoing rehabilitation |
| Laboratory | Basic outpatient testing and context-appropriate RDTs |
| Pharmacy | Outpatient drug supply for declared capacity; tetanus prophylaxis; minimum continuity support for chronic medicines |
| Sterilization | Basic steam autoclave at base or disposable sterile material |
| IPC | IPC protocols for mobile care; hand hygiene; safe waste handling; communicable disease screening/referral |
| Imaging | No diagnostic imaging required |
| ICU | Not applicable |
| Blood transfusion | Not applicable |

## Facility Layout

- Single patient presentation point at each mobile clinic site with patient identification/registration
- Required zones: mobile triage · waiting/separation area · treatment/procedure area · pharmacy/dispensing point · waste/sharps point · staff area
- Base of operations required for resupply, cold chain, sterilization/disposables, communications, WASH support, and team welfare
- Patient flow must allow safe separation of suspected infectious patients and immediate referral where isolation is needed
- Layout must support accessibility, privacy, dignity, culturally appropriate gender separation where needed, and safe staff travel

---

## Triage Requirements

- Established triage system for both daily care and mass casualty incidents (MCI)
- Includes infectious disease screening at triage
- Dedicated triage staff member per shift
- MCI management plan must be validated and rehearsed
- Waiting patients reassessed when clinical condition may change

---

## Referral and Transfer

- Referral is a central Type 1 Mobile function; written protocols required
- Communicate with receiving facility before transfer where possible
- Stabilize using ABCDE and manage pain before transport
- Obtain informed consent; provide written clinical summary with each transfer
- Responsibility remains with transferring team until formal handover to receiving facility
- Mobile teams must know local referral routes from each served location and maintain safe transport/communication arrangements

## Operational Parameters

| Resource | Specification |
|---|---|
| Power | Single 2 kVA petrol generator for clinic-site transport; 5–10 kVA for base camp needs; fuel/reserve for vehicles |
| Water — staff | Hub/base staff: 40–60 L/person/day; field staff planning assumption: 5 L/person/day |
| Water — inpatients | Not applicable |
| Water — outpatients | 5 L/outpatient/day; planning benchmark 50 outpatients/day per mobile team |
| Toilets — staff | Base camp staff latrines 1:20, gender separated; one mobile toilet solution for field visits per team |
| Toilets — patients | Outpatient latrines 1:50 when facilities are provided; consider gender separation, children, PWD access, and menstrual hygiene |
| Infectious waste | For 2 mobile teams: minimum treatment capacity 16.8 kg every two days; each mobile team must safely contain/transport 42 L waste in sealed containers to base operations |
| Mortuary | Not applicable to Type 1 Mobile as a required facility; report deaths and follow local authority procedures |
| Communications | Local SIM/non-smart and smart phones; low-earth-orbit/Iridium-type satellite phone for voice; satellite broadband/data/voice at base camp; one primary plus one backup system |

Additional: Mobile teams must manage/contain their own excreta waste for safe disposal at base camp or approved local system. Staff food, pharmacy, medical consumables, WASH, communications, and logistics must be self-sufficient without burdening local resources.

---

## Staffing

- Minimum staffing is capability-based; the Blue Book gives planning examples rather than a universal full roster
- Mobile field planning example: 2 mobile teams × 4 persons, with a 13-person base of operations
- Staff mix must include personnel competent in emergency/primary care, BLS, triage, basic RMNH/paediatric stabilization, IPC, pharmacy dispensing, records, logistics, communications, and WASH support
- Clinical staff must remain within licensed scope of practice; paramedic roles must be supervised where host-country licensing requires it
- Structured clinical handover between shifts and between mobile team/base operations required

---

## Coordination and Reporting

- Deploy only when formally accepted and tasked by the relevant authority
- Register on arrival per national or EMT coordination procedures
- Operate under the national health emergency management authority and/or EMT Coordination Cell
- Report using national forms; use EMT Minimum Data Set if national forms are unavailable
- Maintain confidential patient records; provide each patient with a discharge or referral document
- Coordinate referrals, transfers, public health reporting, and exit planning with local authorities
- No research without patient consent, national authority approval, and ethical approval
