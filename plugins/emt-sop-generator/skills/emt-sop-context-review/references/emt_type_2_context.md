# EMT Type 2 — Agent Context

_Source: WHO Classification and Minimum Standards for EMTs, 2021_

---

## Identity Parameters

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Type | EMT Type 2 — Inpatient Surgical Emergency Care |
| Facility model | Temporary deployable inpatient surgical facility with outpatient/emergency intake |
| Care level | Type 1 services plus inpatient acute care, general surgery, orthopaedic surgery, obstetric surgery, anaesthesia, diagnostics, blood transfusion, rehabilitation, and follow-up |
| Min throughput | >100 outpatients/day; at least 20 inpatient beds per operating table; at least 1 operating table; 7 major or 15 minor operations/day |
| Operating hours | 24 h/day, 7 days/week |
| Deployment readiness | Operational from arrival to affected area/site within 24–36 h; all EMTs expected to be deployable and operational within 72 h of disaster |
| Min deployment duration | 3 weeks |
| Structure | Temporary, deployable, adaptable field hospital structure with operating theatre, inpatient beds, diagnostics, sterilization, blood transfusion, pharmacy, WASH, power, logistics, and support services |

---

## Scope of Care

**Permitted:**
- Type 1 outpatient/emergency services plus intake and screening of new/referred patients and counter-referral
- Surgical triage, advanced life support, advanced airway management, and initial stabilization
- General emergency surgery, damage-control surgery, emergency orthopaedic surgery, wound surgery, basic fracture management, and amputations when indicated
- C-EmONC including caesarean section and management of major obstetric complications within Type 2 capability
- Inpatient care for medical, communicable, NCD, paediatric, obstetric, surgical, and non-trauma emergencies
- Paediatric/adult regional, spinal, and general anaesthesia; recovery area; X-ray; laboratory; sterilization; safe blood transfusion
- Outpatient and inpatient rehabilitation services and patient follow-up

**Refer to higher care:**
- ICU-level care, mechanical ventilation, complex referral care, or advanced/intensive NCD exacerbations beyond Type 2 capability
- Burns >20% TBSA or burns to face, hands, perineum, genitals, soles, or otherwise requiring specialist burns care
- Complex reconstructive wound care, complex orthopaedic/spinal care, maxillofacial/orthoplastic/neurosurgical care, dialysis, or specialist paediatric/neonatal intensive care
- Cases where required diagnostics, specialist staff, consumables, safe blood, oxygen, or referral continuity cannot be guaranteed

---

## Explicit Exclusions

- No ICU admission or mechanical ventilation as a Type 2 minimum standard
- No Type 3 complex referral-level reconstructive, intensive care, or specialist surgical capability unless separately declared as a specialized care team
- No unmanaged burns >20% TBSA or critical-area burns; refer to Type 3/burns specialist centre where available
- No neurosurgery, dialysis, neonatal ICU, paediatric ICU, or maxillofacial/orthoplastic reconstruction unless separately specialist-equipped and accepted
- No surgical team without full facility and operational support may be described as Type 2
- No inpatient care without 24/7 medical supervision, nursing, pharmacy, WASH, power, oxygen, waste, and referral systems

## Clinical Capabilities

| Domain | Capability |
|---|---|
| Triage | Surgical triage; outpatient/emergency intake; acceptance of referrals; counter-referral; MCI plan |
| Resuscitation | Advanced life support, advanced stabilization, oxygen to multiple patients, blood transfusion, emergency surgery |
| Airway | Advanced airway management including endotracheal intubation and surgical airway with capnography |
| Wounds | Full surgical wound care; debridement, delayed closure, graft/basic flap where appropriate; referral for complex reconstruction |
| Burns | Treat burns up to 20% TBSA; surgical scrub, fluids, dressings, positioning, excision/grafting where appropriate, nutrition, early rehab |
| Fractures | Advanced fracture management/surgery, traction, external fixation, amputation when indicated; follow-up/removal pathway required |
| Spinal injury | Assessment, immobilization, respiratory support and transfer; report suspected spinal injury |
| Communicable disease | Inpatient capacity for communicable disease cases; isolation facilities; separated hygiene/sanitation for isolation; reporting |
| NCDs | Inpatient care of acute exacerbations; refer advanced/intensive cases |
| RMNH | C-EmONC and sexual/reproductive health; assisted vaginal delivery, C-section, manual placenta removal, incomplete abortion management, obstetric complication care |
| Paediatrics | Emergency inpatient/outpatient paediatric care, stabilization, paediatric surgery, management of malnutrition; paediatric anaesthesia capability |
| Analgesia/anaesthesia | Paediatric/adult regional, spinal, and general anaesthesia; procedural sedation; recovery space with monitoring |
| Surgery | General emergency surgical care; at least 1 operating table; 7 major or 15 minor cases/day; at least 5 OT technical staff per OT |
| Palliative care | Symptom control, palliative surgical care, end-of-life support |
| Rehabilitation | Outpatient and inpatient acute rehabilitation; at least 1 rehabilitation professional per 20 beds if staying >3 weeks |
| Laboratory | Basic inpatient testing; blood grouping and transfusion-related infectious disease screening as required for safe blood |
| Pharmacy | Inpatient and outpatient drug supply including surgical and anaesthetic medicines; controlled-drug process |
| Sterilization | Complete surgical autoclave with traceability; temperature/humidity control and power redundancy |
| IPC | Facility-wide IPC protocols; OT/sterilization/isolation IPC; minimum 1 m between beds |
| Imaging | X-ray required; plain radiography for limbs, chest, pelvis, spine; dedicated X-ray space and safety controls |
| ICU | Not applicable as a minimum Type 2 service |
| Blood transfusion | Safe blood transfusion capability required; policies for donation, testing, storage, crossmatch, complications, documentation |

## Facility Layout

- Single entry point with registration, unique patient identifier, screening, triage, and referral/counter-referral desk
- Required zones: triage · emergency/resuscitation · outpatient care · operating theatre · recovery · inpatient wards · maternity/delivery · paediatric area · isolation · X-ray · laboratory · pharmacy · sterilization · rehabilitation · waste · WASH · staff/support/admin
- Operating theatre environment must meet IPC and hygiene requirements; patient flow should minimize OT movement and cross-contamination
- Isolation areas require own access to hygiene facilities, separated sanitation, PPE donning/removal route, and infectious grey-water/waste controls
- Layout must include accessible routes, privacy, protection, gender/cultural considerations, and patient/caregiver dignity

---

## Triage Requirements

- Established triage system for both daily care and mass casualty incidents (MCI)
- Includes infectious disease screening at triage
- Dedicated triage staff member per shift
- MCI management plan must be validated and rehearsed
- Waiting patients reassessed when clinical condition may change

---

## Referral and Transfer

- Referral is a central Type 2 function; written protocols required for accepting referrals, counter-referral, and onward specialist transfer
- Communicate with receiving facility before transfer where possible
- Stabilize using ABCDE and manage pain before transport
- Obtain informed consent; provide written clinical summary with each transfer
- Responsibility remains with transferring team until formal handover to receiving facility
- Critical patients require suitable escort, airway/IV/analgesia/fracture stabilization, and transport equipment before transfer
- Transfer triggers include ICU need, specialist surgery, burns beyond Type 2, complex spinal/orthopaedic reconstruction, dialysis, and services beyond declared capacity

## Operational Parameters

| Resource | Specification |
|---|---|
| Power | 30–50 kVA for standard-sized Type 2 without air conditioning; consider doubling for deployments requiring air conditioning |
| Water — staff | 40–60 L/person/day |
| Water — inpatients | 60 L/inpatient/day |
| Water — outpatients | 5 L/outpatient/day; benchmark >100 outpatients/day |
| Water — surgical/delivery | 100 L per surgical intervention or delivery |
| Toilets — staff | 1:20 staff latrines, gender separated; example 60-person team may require 6 staff latrines after gender separation |
| Toilets — patients | Outpatients 1:50; inpatients 1:20; include isolation toilet, limited-mobility accessible toilet, child-friendly/PWD access, menstrual hygiene, and handwash facilities |
| Infectious waste | Approx. minimum treatment capacity 20 kg/day or 3 kg/hour over 8 h; approximately 200 L infectious hazard containment for 20-bed/100-outpatient planning assumptions |
| Mortuary | At least 4 bodies in a dedicated, temperature-controlled tent/space |
| Communications | Same as Type 1 Mobile plus UHF/VHF radios or walkie-talkies for internal site communication; one primary plus one backup voice/data system |

Additional: Type 2 must provide food for staff, inpatients, and nominated caregivers; maintain pharmacy/medical stock for the deployment period; provide oxygen by cylinders and/or concentrators; maintain pressurized water supply in critical clinical areas; and coordinate demobilization/handover at least 7 days before shutdown.

---

## Staffing

- Minimum overall roster is capability-based; Annex 6 planning assumption uses a 60-person Type 2 team for a 20-bed, 1-OT configuration
- Minimum 1 operating table with at least 5 OT technical staff (nurses or equivalent)
- Surgical service requires licenced surgeons able to perform emergency general/orthopaedic/obstetric procedures, anaesthesiologists, OT nurses/technicians, recovery staff, ward nurses, rehabilitation staff, laboratory/imaging/pharmacy staff, IPC, WASH, logistics, security, administration, and communications staff
- Ward care staffing should support 24/7 supervision; recommended nurse-patient ratio 1:8 per shift and doctor-nurse ratio 1:3
- Anaesthesia/surgery optimal ratios: anaesthesiologist:OT table 2:1, anaesthesiologist:surgeon 1:1, surgeon:OT table 2:1
- Type 2 child health requires paediatric-experienced clinician/nurses and surgeon/anaesthesiologist with paediatric experience
- Structured clinical handover between shifts required

---

## Coordination and Reporting

- Deploy only when formally accepted and tasked by the relevant authority
- Register on arrival per national or EMT coordination procedures
- Operate under the national health emergency management authority and/or EMT Coordination Cell
- Report using national forms; use EMT Minimum Data Set if national forms are unavailable
- Maintain confidential patient records; provide each patient with a discharge or referral document
- Coordinate referrals, transfers, public health reporting, and exit planning with local authorities
- No research without patient consent, national authority approval, and ethical approval
