# EMT Type 1 Fixed — Agent Context

_Source: WHO Classification and Minimum Standards for EMTs, 2021_

---

## Identity Parameters

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Type | EMT Type 1 Fixed |
| Facility model | Fixed outpatient facility |
| Care level | Outpatient initial emergency + primary care |
| Min throughput | 100 outpatients/day |
| Operating hours | Daytime; on-call overnight for life-saving emergencies only |
| Deployment readiness | Operational within 24 h of arrival at field site |
| Min deployment duration | 2 weeks |
| Structure | Tents, equipped vehicles, or suitable existing structures |

---

## Scope of Care

**Permitted:**
- Triage, first aid, basic resuscitation, stabilization (ABCDE)
- Minor trauma and non-trauma emergencies
- Basic outpatient care for communicable and non-communicable diseases
- Basic reproductive, maternal, and newborn health
- Basic paediatric care, stabilization, malnutrition screening + ambulatory treatment
- Initial wound care; basic fracture management; superficial burns ≤5% TBSA
- Pain control and local anaesthesia; minor outpatient procedures
- Basic rehabilitation care; initial palliative care with referral
- Isolation for suspected communicable disease cases
- CBRN: recognition, first aid, decontamination where possible, referral

**Refer to higher care:**
- Surgery, inpatient care, ICU, complex obstetrics, burns >5% TBSA, spinal care, advanced diagnostics

---

## Explicit Exclusions

- No inpatient/ward care (observation only while awaiting referral)
- No operating theatre or surgical capability
- No blood transfusion
- No ICU or assisted ventilation
- No diagnostic imaging required; X-ray is not a minimum standard
- Ultrasound: optional only, not a minimum standard
- No advanced or complex referral-level care

---

## Facility Layout

- Single entry point; unique patient identifier system
- Required zones: triage · waiting · consultation/treatment · observation · pharmacy · isolation · referral/transfer
- Dedicated maternal/newborn stabilization or emergency delivery area
- Infectious patients physically separated from general patient flow
- Privacy, dignity, and accessibility maintained for all groups (children, women, elderly, people with disabilities)

---

## Triage Requirements

- Established triage system for both daily care and mass casualty incidents (MCI)
- Includes infectious disease screening at triage
- Dedicated triage staff member per shift
- MCI management plan must be validated and rehearsed
- Waiting patients reassessed when clinical condition may change

---

## Referral and Transfer

- Referral is a central Type 1 Fixed function; written protocols required
- Communicate with receiving facility before transfer where possible
- Stabilize using ABCDE and manage pain before transport
- Obtain informed consent; provide written clinical summary with each transfer
- Responsibility remains with transferring team until formal handover to receiving facility

## Operational Parameters

| Resource | Specification |
|---|---|
| Power | 5–10 kVA |
| Water — staff | 40–60 L/person/day |
| Water — outpatients | 5 L/outpatient/day |
| Toilets — staff | 1 per 20 persons |
| Toilets — outpatients | 1 per 50 persons |
| Infectious waste | Min 10 kg/day treatment capacity; 100 L hazard containment |
| Mortuary | 2 bodies in a dedicated tent |
| Communications | 1 primary + 1 backup system; SIM-enabled + satellite backup; voice and data |

Additional: at least one toilet supporting menstrual hygiene needs; accessible sanitation for people with disabilities.

---

## Staffing

- Minimum 3 doctors trained in emergency and primary care
- Medical supervision 24/7 — physical presence or on-call system
- Structured clinical handover between shifts required

---

## Coordination and Reporting

- Deploy only when formally accepted and tasked by the relevant authority
- Register on arrival per national or EMT coordination procedures
- Operate under the national health emergency management authority and/or EMT Coordination Cell
- Report using national forms; use EMT Minimum Data Set if national forms are unavailable
- Maintain confidential patient records; provide each patient with a discharge or referral document
- Coordinate referrals, transfers, public health reporting, and exit planning with local authorities
- No research without patient consent, national authority approval, and ethical approval
