# EMT Type 3 — Agent Context

_Source: WHO Classification and Minimum Standards for EMTs, 2021_

---

## Identity Parameters

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Type | EMT Type 3 — Inpatient Referral Care |
| Facility model | Temporary referral-level field hospital with inpatient, surgical, specialist, and intensive care capacity |
| Care level | Type 2 services plus complex referral care, complex reconstructive/orthopaedic care, specialist services, ICU, and assisted ventilation |
| Min throughput | >100 outpatients/day and >40 inpatients; at least 2 operating tables; at least 40 inpatient beds (20 beds/OT); 15 major or 30 minor operations/day; at least 4 ICU beds |
| Operating hours | 24 h/day, 7 days/week |
| Deployment readiness | Operational from arrival to affected area/site within 36–48 h; all EMTs expected to be deployable and operational within 72 h of disaster |
| Min deployment duration | 4 weeks |
| Structure | Self-contained field hospital or equivalent temporary facility using tents, prefabricated buildings, or vehicles; must provide its own structures and full operational support |

---

## Scope of Care

**Permitted:**
- All Type 2 services plus high-level referral care for Type 1 and Type 2 teams and local facilities
- Complex referral triage, advanced stabilization, intensive care-level management, assisted ventilation, and ICU specialist care
- Complex reconstructive wound care, definitive/complex orthopaedic care, specialized trauma and reconstructive surgery, including orthoplastic and maxillofacial capability where declared
- Adult and paediatric anaesthesia, intermediate/post-operative ventilation, advanced monitoring, and ICU-level perioperative care
- Care of critically ill children, complex paediatric surgical care, neonatal/paediatric intensive care capability where declared
- Advanced laboratory testing, X-ray, eFAST ultrasound, intensive-care-level pharmacy, complete sterilization, blood transfusion, inpatient rehabilitation for complex trauma

**Refer to higher care:**
- Any service not declared, staffed, equipped, licensed, or safe within the Type 3 configuration
- Subspecialty services beyond team capability, such as neurosurgery, dialysis, burns specialist centre care, or specialist toxicology/CBRN care unless separately deployed as a specialized care team
- Patients whose required ongoing care exceeds deployment duration, ethical triage criteria, discharge criteria, or safe continuity of care

---

## Explicit Exclusions

- No assumption that Type 3 provides every subspecialty; only declared and accepted specialist services may be described
- No neurosurgical, dialysis, burns specialist, CBRN specialist, or highly infectious disease specialist service unless specifically configured and accepted
- No ICU admission without 24/7 trained staff, monitoring, oxygen, ventilators, power redundancy, medicines, and exit strategy
- No forensic pathology, disaster victim identification, or mass fatality storage function
- No internal fixation/bone grafting in inappropriate tented disaster settings; use only facilities meeting appropriate surgical standards
- No unmanaged referral overload; admission/referral criteria and ceilings of care must be explicit

## Clinical Capabilities

| Domain | Capability |
|---|---|
| Triage | Complex referral triage; acceptance of Type 1/2/local referrals; MCI referral triage; admission/exclusion criteria |
| Resuscitation | Intensive care-level management, advanced stabilization, assisted ventilation, blood transfusion, emergency surgery |
| Airway | Advanced airway management, endotracheal intubation, surgical airway, capnography, mechanical ventilation in ICU |
| Wounds | Complex reconstructive wound care, serial debridement, orthoplastic/maxillofacial capability where declared |
| Burns | Treat all burns >20% TBSA and burns to face, hands, perineum, genitals, soles; burns specialist team may still be required |
| Fractures | Definitive and complex orthopaedic care; complex limb injury care; external fixation and reconstruction where appropriate |
| Spinal injury | Complex spinal care; prevent complications; referral/consultation for long-term specialist pathway as needed |
| Communicable disease | Complex cases requiring intensive care; isolation/cohorting with separate access to hygiene and sanitation |
| NCDs | Advanced/intensive care management of emergency exacerbations |
| RMNH | C-EmONC and sexual/reproductive health with intensive care support; maternal/neonatal intensive support where configured |
| Paediatrics | Critically ill children, complex paediatric surgery, paediatric and neonatal intensive care |
| Analgesia/anaesthesia | Adult/paediatric regional, spinal, general anaesthesia; extended post-operative/intermediate care including ventilation; advanced monitoring |
| Surgery | Specialized and advanced trauma/reconstructive surgical care; at least 2 OR tables; 15 major or 30 minor operations/day |
| Palliative care | Symptom control, palliative surgical care, end-of-life care support, multidisciplinary ceilings-of-care decisions |
| Rehabilitation | Outpatient and inpatient rehabilitation for complex trauma; rehabilitation specialist integrated into care pathway |
| Laboratory | Advanced testing; accept samples from smaller teams; blood gas, electrolytes, renal function, FBC, culture/sensitivity, Gram stain/microscopy |
| Pharmacy | Intensive-care-level drug supply, anaesthetic agents, opioids/analgesia, controlled-drug systems, emergency medicines |
| Sterilization | Complete surgical autoclave with traceability; temperature/humidity control; power redundancy |

| ICU | At least 4 ICU beds and at least 1 incubator; 24/7 monitoring; mechanical ventilation; 1:2 ICU nurse-patient ratio per shift |
| Blood transfusion | Safe blood transfusion capability required with donor/patient testing, storage, crossmatch, complication management, documentation |

## Facility Layout

- Single controlled entry with registration, screening, complex triage, admission/referral decision point, and referral coordination
- Required zones: triage · emergency/resuscitation · outpatient · operating theatres · recovery/PACU · ICU · inpatient wards · maternity · paediatrics/neonatal · isolation/cohorting · X-ray/eFAST · laboratory · transfusion/blood storage · pharmacy · sterilization · rehabilitation · mortuary · waste · WASH · logistics/warehouse · staff/admin
- ICU, OT, sterilization, laboratory, pharmacy, and isolation require power, oxygen, ventilation/climate, IPC, and controlled access
- Patient flow must separate clean/dirty routes, infectious/non-infectious pathways, surgical/ICU flows, and public/staff/logistics circulation
- Layout must support accessibility, dignity, privacy, caregiver management, gender/cultural considerations, and safe night movement

---

## Triage Requirements

- Established triage system for both daily care and mass casualty incidents (MCI)
- Includes infectious disease screening at triage
- Dedicated triage staff member per shift
- MCI management plan must be validated and rehearsed
- Waiting patients reassessed when clinical condition may change

---

## Referral and Transfer

- Referral is a central Type 3 function; written protocols required for receiving complex referrals and referring beyond declared capability
- Communicate with receiving facility before transfer where possible
- Stabilize using ABCDE and manage pain before transport
- Obtain informed consent; provide written clinical summary with each transfer
- Responsibility remains with transferring team until formal handover to receiving facility
- Define ICU admission/exclusion, discharge, transfer, and ceilings-of-care criteria before operations
- Accept referrals from Type 1/2 teams and local facilities only when service, staffing, equipment, continuity, and ethical triage criteria are met

## Operational Parameters

| Resource | Specification |
|---|---|
| Power | Estimate 100–200 kVA depending on air conditioning and specialist systems; redundancy required for life-saving equipment |
| Water — staff | 40–60 L/person/day |
| Water — inpatients | 60 L/inpatient/day; benchmark >40 inpatients, with larger configurations scaled to declared bed capacity |
| Water — outpatients | 5 L/outpatient/day; benchmark >100 outpatients/day; Annex example uses 200 outpatients/day |
| Water — surgical/delivery | 100 L per surgical intervention or delivery; Annex example uses 30 surgical interventions/day |
| Toilets — staff | 1:20 staff latrines, gender separated; scale for 24/7 hospital workforce |
| Toilets — patients | Outpatients 1:50; inpatients 1:20; include inpatient, outpatient, isolation, limited-mobility accessible, child-friendly/PWD, MHM, and handwashing facilities |
| Infectious waste | Minimum technology at least 40 kg/day; scale to declared bed/OPD load; large Annex example requires about 10 kg/hour treatment and about 768 L containment |
| Mortuary | At least 4 bodies in a dedicated, temperature-controlled tent/space |
| Communications | Same as Type 2; consider high-bandwidth data connections for telehealth/remote specialist support; one primary plus one backup voice/data system |

Additional: Type 3 requires pressurized water supply, oxygen through cylinders/concentrators or central installation/PSA/liquid systems, ICU power redundancy/UPS, blood cold chain, staff/patient/caregiver food, advanced pharmacy, WASH, warehouse, security, and full demobilization/handover planning.

---

## Staffing

- Minimum overall roster is capability-based; Annex 6 planning assumption uses an 80–100 person Type 3 team depending configuration
- Minimum ICU capacity: at least 4 ICU beds and at least 1 incubator; ICU staffing must include qualified intensivists and ICU-trained nurses at 1:2 nurse-patient ratio per shift
- Minimum surgical platform: at least 2 operating tables with surgeons, anaesthesiologists, OT nurses/technicians, recovery staff, sterilization staff, blood/transfusion support, and logistics
- Anaesthesia/surgery optimal ratios: anaesthesiologist:OT table 2:1, anaesthesiologist:surgeon 1:1, surgeon:OT table 2:1; consider additional anaesthesiologist outside OT assignment
- Paediatric/neonatal intensive care capability requires paediatric emergency doctor, paediatric surgeon, paediatric anaesthesiologist, and neonatal intensive-care-experienced nurse
- Rehabilitation specialists, laboratory, imaging, pharmacy, IPC, WASH, biomedical, power, oxygen, communications, safety/security, records, admin, and logistics staff required for 24/7 referral-level operations
- Structured clinical handover between shifts required

---

## Coordination and Reporting

- Deploy only when formally accepted and tasked by the relevant authority
- Register on arrival per national or EMT coordination procedures
- Operate under the national health emergency management authority and/or EMT Coordination Cell
- Report using national forms; use EMT Minimum Data Set if national forms are unavailable
- Maintain confidential patient records; provide each patient with a discharge or referral document
- Coordinate referrals, transfers, public health reporting, and exit planning with local authorities
- No research without patient consent, national authority approval, and ethical approval
