# EMT Specialized Care Teams — Agent Context

_Source: WHO Classification and Minimum Standards for EMTs, 2021_

---

## Identity Parameters

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Type | Specialized Care Team / Special EMT |
| Facility model | Modular specialty team embedded into existing local health-care facilities or Type 2/Type 3 EMTs unless specified otherwise; may bring its own structures when declared “with facilities” |
| Care level | Specialty-specific surge support: outbreak, surgical, rehabilitation, mental health, RMNH/child health, interdisciplinary, interhospital, laboratory/transfusion, pharmacy/donation, coordination, or other technical support |
| Min throughput | Variable; team must declare measurable capacity before deployment (e.g., beds, operations/day, dialysis interventions/day, tests/day, transports/day, vaccination throughput, ward beds, ICU beds) |
| Operating hours | Variable by specialty and host tasking; must match declared service and continuity requirements |
| Deployment readiness | Variable by specialty; rapid modules should declare activation and operational timelines |
| Min deployment duration | Variable by specialty; surgical general teams should generally not deploy for <2 weeks; rehabilitation and complex inpatient specialties often require longer periods |
| Structure | Embedded module, add-on tent/ward, adapted facility space, mobile/interhospital platform, or standalone specialty facility when declared; must bring equipment and consumables for the full deployment |

---

## Scope of Care

**Permitted:**
- Provide only the declared specialty service accepted by local health authorities/EMTCC
- Embed into local health facilities, Type 2, or Type 3 teams to fill specific clinical or technical gaps
- Bring specialty staff, equipment, consumables, logistics, and WASH/IPC support needed for the declared service
- Support outbreak response, IPC mentoring, DTC/ORS setup, VHF/respiratory/vector-borne/vaccination/burial functions where specialized and accepted
- Support surgical specialties, burns, orthoplastic/reconstructive, neurosurgical support, rehabilitation, spinal care, mental health/PSS, maternal-newborn-child health, ward care, nutrition, dialysis, CBRN, prehospital/interhospital, medevac, laboratory/transfusion, pharmacy/donation, and EMTCC support where declared

**Refer to higher care:**
- Any patient or function outside the declared specialty, licensing, equipment, staffing, or host-facility capability
- Any case where the host facility lacks safe power, oxygen, water, sterilization, transfusion, waste, ward, ICU, or referral continuity required for the specialty service
- Complex or intensive care needs beyond the host facility or receiving EMT type unless the specialized team explicitly provides that capability
- Public health, protection, nutrition, WASH, or community needs beyond medical module scope to the relevant authority/cluster/service

---

## Explicit Exclusions

- No generic Type 1, Type 2, or Type 3 status unless the whole team meets that type’s minimum standards
- No surgical team without facilities may be described as Type 2 or Type 3; it is a specialized surgical care team
- No service outside declared and accepted specialty scope
- No independent operation outside national health authority/EMTCC tasking
- No reliance on host facility power, water, oxygen, sterilization, transfusion, ward, or waste systems without prior verification and redundancy/alternate measures if patient safety may be compromised
- No research or clinical trials without patient consent, national authority approval, and ethical approval
- No direct mental health intervention by international teams without adaptation to local language, context, culture, and coordination with local professionals

## Clinical Capabilities

| Domain | Capability |
|---|---|
| Triage | Specialty-specific; integrate with host facility triage and referral pathways; outbreak teams require screening/streaming systems |
| Resuscitation | Specialty-specific; surgical/prehospital/ICU modules must declare ALS, airway, oxygen, transfusion, and critical-care capacity if provided |
| Airway | Only if declared and staffed; surgical/ICU/prehospital modules must bring trained personnel and equipment |
| Wounds | Surgical/orthoplastic/burns/rehab modules may provide advanced wound care; otherwise host/Type 2/3 responsibility |
| Burns | Burns specialist teams may support large numbers of severe burns patients, surgical interventions, anaesthesia, nursing, rehab, and logistics |
| Fractures | Orthopaedic/orthoplastic teams may provide advanced fracture/limb injury care when embedded into suitable surgical/ward systems |
| Spinal injury | Spinal care teams support existing spinal centres, referral hospitals, or Type 3; advise surrounding facilities/EMTs |
| Communicable disease | Outbreak teams may provide DTC/ORS, respiratory expansion wards, VHF treatment units, IPC support, vaccination, or burial support |
| NCDs | Specialty-specific; ward/dialysis/prehospital modules may support NCD continuity or acute complication pathways |
| RMNH | Maternal/newborn/child modules support facilities with pre-existing RMNH services and Type 2/3 EMTs |
| Paediatrics | Paediatric modules support child-heavy emergencies/outbreaks and may combine with ICU, maternal/newborn, or surgical teams |
| Analgesia/anaesthesia | Surgical teams must bring anaesthetist(s); anaesthesia capability must match declared procedures |
| Surgery | Specialized surgical teams: at least 1 specialist surgeon, 1 anaesthetist per surgeon, 3 nurses per surgeon, and 1 logistician; general surgical teams aim for 7 major or 15 minor operations/day |
| Palliative care | Specialty-specific; must coordinate with host facility and cultural/community practices |
| Rehabilitation | Rehab teams can bolster Type 2/3 or local facilities; should plan for extended deployment and acute/long-term continuity |
| Laboratory | Laboratory/transfusion modules may supplement/replace damaged services; must declare tests/day and maintain ≥2 weeks reagents/self-sufficiency |
| Pharmacy | Pharmacy/donation teams support reception, storage, distribution, donation compliance, and stock information systems |
| Sterilization | Specialty-specific; surgical teams must verify host sterilization or bring redundancy/alternate supplies |
| IPC | Outbreak/IPC teams provide PPE, triage, mentoring, water/handwashing support, and embedded practice improvement |
| Imaging | Specialty-specific; only if declared and staffed/equipped |
| ICU | ICU modules may add declared ICU beds to a host facility/EMT; must bring staff, beds, consumables, power/oxygen assumptions, and exit strategy |
| Blood transfusion | Transfusion modules must meet Type 2/3 transfusion standards; surgical teams must verify host transfusion capacity or bring redundancy |

## Facility Layout

- Entry and patient identification usually follow host facility or receiving EMT process; specialized teams must integrate into that flow
- Required zones depend on declared module: specialty care zone · staff/support zone · supplies/warehouse point · waste/sharps point · documentation/reporting point · referral/handover point
- “With facilities” modules must provide appropriate clinical spaces, WASH, IPC, waste, water, power, oxygen, sanitation, and patient flow for their declared service
- Outbreak modules require clear screening, clean/dirty flows, PPE donning/doffing, isolation/cohorting, waste, laundry/cleaning, and community engagement routes
- Surgical modules must verify OR, recovery, ward, blood, sterilization, oxygen, water, and power before operating
- Layout must support accessibility, dignity, privacy, gender/cultural considerations, and host-facility continuity

---

## Triage Requirements

- Established triage system for daily care and MCI must be used by the host facility/receiving EMT and understood by the specialized team
- Includes infectious disease screening where relevant to the specialty
- Dedicated triage/streaming role required for outbreak, prehospital, surgical surge, ward, or high-volume modules
- MCI management plan must be validated and rehearsed when the module contributes to MCI care
- Waiting or referred patients must be reassessed when clinical condition may change

---

## Referral and Transfer

- Referral is a central Specialized Care Team function; written protocols required for interface with host facility, Type 2/3 EMT, local services, and EMTCC
- Communicate with receiving facility before transfer where possible
- Stabilize using ABCDE and manage pain before transport when the team provides clinical care
- Obtain informed consent; provide written clinical summary with each transfer or specialty handover
- Responsibility remains with transferring team until formal handover to receiving facility or designated transport service
- Specialized teams must declare acceptance/refusal criteria, capacity limits, onward referral triggers, and continuity plan before clinical operations

## Operational Parameters

| Resource | Specification |
|---|---|
| Power | Variable; verify host supply and bring redundancy/alternate measures for specialty-critical equipment; apply Type 1/2/3 assumptions if operating own facility |
| Water — staff | 40–60 L/person/day unless fully supported by verified host facility arrangements |
| Water — inpatients | 60 L/inpatient/day if the module creates inpatient beds |
| Water — outpatients | 5 L/outpatient/day if the module provides outpatient care |
| Water — surgical/delivery | 100 L per surgical intervention or delivery if applicable |
| Toilets — staff | 1:20 staff latrines, gender separated, if providing own facilities; otherwise verify host access does not overload local services |
| Toilets — patients | Outpatients 1:50 and inpatients 1:20 when providing own patient areas; include PWD access, isolation toilet, MHM, and handwashing as relevant |
| Infectious waste | Variable; calculate from declared service volume; outbreak/surgical/lab/transfusion modules require dedicated segregation, containment, treatment, and PPE |
| Mortuary | Variable; not universal. Outbreak burial/body-management teams follow dead-body management specialty procedures; facility modules follow Type 1/2/3 body-storage standards as applicable |
| Communications | One primary plus one backup voice/data system; integrate with host facility, EMTCC, referral, safety/security, and reporting channels |

Additional: Self-sufficiency is mandatory for clinical supplies, equipment, consumables, logistics, power/communications, staff welfare, and WASH aspects unless verified host support is safe and accepted. National teams generally require at least 3 days self-sufficiency; international EMTs require at least 14 days with resupply mechanism.

---

## Staffing

- Staffing is specialty-specific and must be declared in the offer of assistance
- Surgical specialist team: at least 1 specialist surgeon, 1 anaesthetist per surgeon, 3 nurses per surgeon, and 1 logistician
- General surgical specialist teams should generally deploy for at least 2 weeks and aim for 7 major or 15 minor operations/day when declaring that capability
- Dialysis team: at least 2 nephrologists and 4 nephrology nurses plus logistics staff; machines, consumables, and dialysate for declared capacity for at least 2 weeks
- Prehospital/interhospital team: prehospital-trained doctors, nurses and/or paramedics; minimum 2 doctors for oversight where paramedic licensing may not exist
- Ward care teams: nursing staff for day/night shifts, plus small medical staff numbers such as 1–2 generalist internal medicine staff and context-specific specialists
- Paediatric, RMNH, ICU, mental health, IPC, lab/transfusion, pharmacy/donation, rehabilitation, and coordination teams require qualified specialist staff plus logistics and records/reporting capacity
- Structured clinical and operational handover between shifts and with host facility required

---

## Coordination and Reporting

- Deploy only when formally accepted and tasked by the relevant authority
- Register on arrival per national or EMT coordination procedures
- Operate under the national health emergency management authority and/or EMT Coordination Cell
- Report using national forms; use EMT Minimum Data Set if national forms are unavailable
- Maintain confidential patient records; provide each patient with a discharge or referral document
- Coordinate referrals, transfers, public health reporting, and exit planning with local authorities
- No research without patient consent, national authority approval, and ethical approval
