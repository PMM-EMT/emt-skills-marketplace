# EMT SOP Drafting Rules

## Capability Boundary

Before authorizing any service, procedure, diagnostic, admission, surgery, critical care function, blood product use, specialist care, transport, public-health function, or operational activity, establish the team's approved capability boundary from user-provided sources.

Ask for the official capability statement, approved clinical and operational scope, exclusions, staffing model, site or mobile operating model, referral and escalation pathways, authorized diagnostics, procedures, medicines, equipment, services, deployment configuration, assigned tasking, and host-country or mission-approved scope changes.

Use this boundary throughout the SOP. Do not rely on a generic EMT type label alone, and do not draft instructions outside the stated mandate, local authorization, staff competence, equipment, medicine availability, and mission tasking.

## Workflow Boundaries

Keep each SOP bound to one primary workflow and deployment phase. If setup, daily operation, maintenance, shift handover, demobilization, or incident follow-up create different triggers, evidence, roles, or escalation thresholds, split them into separate SOPs or working drafts.

## Role-Bound Procedure Pattern

| Drafting need | Required pattern |
| --- | --- |
| Critical actions | Start with the responsible role, then state trigger, action, evidence or output, form or appendix, escalation path, and completion criterion. |
| Procedure steps | Use numbered steps, `If...then...` logic for thresholds, and `Do not...unless...` for safety boundaries. |
| Evidence | Select workflow-matched records such as readiness checklists, rosters, patient records, referral forms, stock cards, cold-chain logs, waste logs, reports, incident logs, handover checklists, and demobilization records. |
| Escalation | Centralize escalation/reporting paths in the escalation subsection; for each threshold name the trigger, responsible role, recipient, evidence, immediate limit or workaround, and phase-specific scope. |
| Tables | Use tables for role ownership, legal matrices, forms, escalation thresholds, and appendices. |

Avoid vague terms such as `appropriate staff`, `if necessary`, `regularly`, or `according to local procedures` unless the role, threshold, frequency, authority, or source is named.

## Terms And Roles

- Define every acronym, role, locally binding term, patient category, report name, authority, coordination body, form, and operational status that affects action, safety, interpretation, reporting, or legal compliance.
- Reference defined terms and role names exactly as written in `Terms and definitions` and `Roles and responsibilities`, translated consistently into the SOP language.
- Add any new term, acronym, authority, form, or role to the appropriate definitions or responsibilities section before finalizing.
- Do not use informal synonyms for defined roles unless those synonyms are separately defined.

## Sources And Appendices

- Capture baseline assumptions from user-provided source material before drafting, including team type, staffing model, operating model, minimum equipment or systems, redundancy assumptions, local authorization dependencies, and known exclusions.
- When a technical, clinical, security, logistics, or legal requirement comes from a source pack or guidebook, convert it into concise field actions, parameters, evidence, or appendix rows. Do not bury source-derived controls in long explanatory paragraphs.
- Keep main SOP procedures concise. Move long resource lists, detailed controls, logs, checklists, contact templates, handover tools, and source-backed tables into appendices ordered by first use.
- Do not hide mandatory actions only in appendices. The main procedure must tell staff when to use each appendix.
- Use product names only when the SOP depends on that product, authorization, configuration, or test; otherwise use generic system names.

## Coordination And Contacts

- Define coordination bodies, technical coordination structures, diplomatic contact points, and local authorities when they appear in the SOP, including mission-specific bodies such as EMTCC, ETC, UCPMT, MoH, EOC, embassies, EU delegation, or equivalent local structures.
- Add contact placeholders only where the procedure depends on reaching coordination, referral, security, emergency, or diplomatic contacts; keep contact templates in appendices unless they are essential in the procedure hot path.

## ICT, Data, And Security

For ICT, data, communications, or other system workflows, separate high-level operational requirements from detailed controls.

- Keep essential safety or separation rules in the main SOP, such as separating clinical-administrative systems from staff welfare or private-use systems; move detailed cybersecurity, device, account, credential, incident, and technical parameters into appendices.
- State incident documentation requirements for data breach, lost device, unauthorized access, or similar events when the workflow creates data or network risk.
