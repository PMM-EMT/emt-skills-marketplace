# EMT abbreviations and role glossary
Source analysed: `blue_book.md` — WHO *Classification and minimum standards for emergency medical teams* (2021).

Purpose: provide AI agents generating EMT SOPs with a consistent glossary of abbreviations, full versions and concise operational meaning.

Scope note: this glossary combines the Blue Book’s official abbreviation section with additional acronyms and role-related terms used elsewhere in the document; obvious OCR artifacts, URL fragments and pure bibliographic codes were excluded. The apparent `AB0` variant in the source text is normalized to `ABO`.

## Role-related terms for SOP generation

| Term / role | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Affected country / host country | The country receiving EMT support and retaining sovereign authority to accept, reject, register, task and coordinate responding teams. |
| Candidate team | An EMT applying for Global Classification or preparing evidence that it meets the relevant EMT standards. |
| Donor | A government, institution or partner providing financial or in-kind support to the EMT Initiative or to EMT capacity strengthening. |
| EMT focal point | The designated national, organizational or team-level contact who acts as the primary counterpart for the regional or global EMT Secretariat. |
| EMT Global Secretariat | The WHO-hosted global coordination function responsible for safeguarding standards, supporting Member States, managing Global Classification and convening technical work. |
| EMT Regional Secretariat | The WHO regional support function that coordinates regional EMT activities, supports teams and mentors, and adapts EMT Initiative work to the regional context. |
| EMT Regional Group | A regional forum where Member States, EMTs and stakeholders guide and drive implementation of the EMT Initiative within a WHO region. |
| EMTCC coordinator / coordination staff | Personnel supporting registration, tasking, information management, referral coordination, reporting and field monitoring of EMTs. |
| IPC focal point | The team member responsible for overseeing infection prevention and control procedures and adherence within the EMT facility. |
| Mentor | An expert assigned to support a candidate team through the Global Classification process and advise on meeting minimum standards. |
| National health authority / ministry of health | The lead national authority responsible for health emergency response, licensing, coordination and integration of EMTs into the health system. |
| Peer reviewer | An external reviewer who assesses whether a candidate EMT complies with guiding principles, core standards and technical standards during verification. |
| Pharmacy focal point | A trained person responsible for pharmacy processes when a registered pharmacist is not included, especially in Type 2 contexts. |
| Security focal point | The team member leading local security risk assessment, security planning, briefings and risk updates during operations. |
| SAG member | A member of the Strategic Advisory Group contributing to global policy, strategy, monitoring and priorities for the EMT Initiative. |
| Team leader | The person responsible for overall deployed team management, operational decision-making and liaison with authorities and coordination mechanisms. |
| Technical expert | A subject-matter specialist who supports classification, standards interpretation or operational problem-solving in clinical, logistics or WASH areas. |
| TWG member | A technical working group expert who contributes evidence, guidance and standards in a defined clinical, operational or policy area. |

## SOP-agent usage notes

- Use the abbreviation only after defining it on first use in an SOP section, unless the abbreviation is universally expected in EMT operations (for example, EMT, WHO, WASH, IPC or PPE).
- Prefer the Blue Book form for official EMT terms, especially EMT, EMTCC, N-EMT, I-EMT, MDS, SAG, TWG and WASH.
- When writing patient-facing or locally translated SOP materials, expand clinical and protection abbreviations such as SGBV, PEP, SAM, TBSA and MHM.
- Do not use abbreviations from this glossary to imply a service capability unless that capability matches the EMT type and declared scope.
